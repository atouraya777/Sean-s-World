From Proverbs there are 5 words or terms that always catch my eye:

  • Discretion
  • Understanding
  • Wisdom
  • Instruction
  • Knowledge

In the Book of Proverbs, these five elements function as an integrated system for godly living, rooted in the "fear of the Lord". Instruction (reception of truth) and knowledge (facts/awareness) feed into understanding (discernment) and wisdom (application), which ultimately produce discretion (protection from evil and folly).

How They Work Together

  • Foundation (Instruction & Knowledge): Instruction involves accepting discipline and wisdom (Proverbs 1:8). Knowledge, or the understanding of God's Word, acts as the raw material for a righteous life.

Torn Between Good and Evil

  • Ambivalent – Someone torn between wanting to improve and feeling indifferent when they slip. It captures that emotional contradiction.
  • Self-sabotaging – Implies a pattern of undermining one’s own goals, often unconsciously or with resignation.
  • Disillusioned – Suggests someone who once had hope or drive but now feels detached or jaded.
  • Wayward – A poetic term for someone who strays from their intended path, often repeatedly.
  • Resigned – Indicates someone who’s given up trying to change, even if they once cared deeply.
  • Inert – More abstract, but evokes a sense of stuckness—wanting change but lacking momentum or emotional investment.
