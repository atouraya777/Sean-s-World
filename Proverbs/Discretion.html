Discretion in the Book of Proverbs

In the Book of Proverbs, discretion (mezimmah in Hebriew) refers to the ability to make wise, thoughtful desicions by thinking ahead, exercising self-control, and anticipating consequences. It is the practical application of wisdom that guards against sin, folly, and danger.

  • Key Characteristics: It is often paired with "prudence" and "understanding," acting as a shield to prevent rash words, harmful actions, and emotional outbursts.
  • Application in Proverbs:
    • Proverbs 2:11: Discretion acts as a guardian, watching over and protecting you.
    • Proverbs 19:11: It provides patience, enabling one to overlook offenses rather than acting in anger.
    • Proverbs 5:2: It helps maintain knowledge, preventing one from falling into traps like immorality.
    • Proverbs 11:22: It highlights that internal wisdom and good judgment are far more valuable than outward beauty
  • Contrast: Discretion is the opposite of the being simple-minded, foolish, or impulsive. It is rooted in the "fear of the Lord", meaning it involves chossing God's way over immediate, selfish desires.

Essentially, biblical discretion is the insight that helps you choose the best, most godly course of action to avoid negative consequences.
