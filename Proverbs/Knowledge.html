Knowledge in The Book of Proverbs

In Proverbs, knowledge (da'ath) signifies a practical, experimental understanding of truth and moral reality rooted in a reverent relationship with God. It is not merely intellectual, but a "skill in godly living" that begins with the fear of the LORD and results in moral, righteous, and wise behavior.

Key Aspects of Knowledge in Proverbs:

  • Foundation in Fear of the LORD: "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" (Proverbs 1:7), meaning true knowledge starts with humility, awe, and submission to God's authority.
  • Experimental and Practical: Knowledge is not just mental, but involves applying truth to real life, often acquired through experience and instruction.
  • Distinction from Wisdom: While closely related, knowledge often refers to understanding facts and God's ways, whereas wisdom is the practical application of that knowledge.
  • Moral and Relational: It involves knowing God intimately and distinguishing good from evil, rather than just accumulating facts.
  • Value: It is portrayed as more valuable than precious gems or gold.

In contrast, a fool despises this knowledge and instruction.
