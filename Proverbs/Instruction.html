Instruction in the Book of Proverbs

In Proverbs, "instruction" (Hebrew: musar) primarily means discpline, correction, training, and wisdom-based guidance. It is a fatherly or divine teaching intended to build moral character, produce, ritcheousness, and prevent foolish mistakes. It is often described as a, tangible, valuable, and necessary discipline to be embraced for a successful life.

Key aspects of instruction in Proverbs include:

  • Discipline and Correction: Musar implies that instruction is often corrective, helping a person turn away from folly and toward wisdom.
  • Active Application: It requires, "taking fast hold" or, "applying the heart," meaning it is not just passive listening but active, obedient, life-guiding, behavior.
  • Purpose: The goal is to develop wisdom, justice, judgment, and equity.
  • Context: It is often presented as the, "instruction of a father" or, "wisdom" calling out, setting it within a framework of parental guidance or, divine wisdom.

Proverbs 4:13 highlights its importance: "Take fast hold of instruction; let her not go: keep her; for she is thy life".
